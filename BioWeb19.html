Why Bio?

Bioengineering is combining the studies of biology and engineering. Within this branch there are many job opportunities ranging from researching and creating theories to becoming a surgeon and performing the surgeries created by the researchers. This field was created right before World War Two and has been rapidly growing since. To become a bioengineer, you have to be proficient in many subfields of science including: mass and heat transfer, kinetics, biocatalysts, biomechanics, bioinformatics, separation and purification processes, bioreactor design, surface science, fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, and polymer science. In conclusion, bioengineering is a newer field of science that has a ton of room for innovation and discovery.


What Jobs Involve Bioengineering?

Health Care In this field, you will be designing medical instruments, prosthetic limbs and even artificial organs. You will most likely work on a team with a chemist and other medical doctors. The average salary for a biomedical engineer is about $91,000.
Laboratories Another job option when going into bioengineering is being a researcher in a lab. Your research could be on a wide variety of topics including how diseases spread and how to stop them. The average salary for this branch is about $75,000.
Surgeon Some people who major in this subject decide to become surgeons and perform corrective and re constructive surgeries that previous researchers have designed to fix or correct a issue in the body. An example would be a ACL repair. The average salary for this job is around $187,200 and can pay up to $314,000 a year.
